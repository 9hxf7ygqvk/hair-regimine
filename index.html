⚠ Scalp Context — Read First

Your primary condition is likely seborrheic dermatitis — flaking + excess oil is the classic marker. The scabs at the nape that burn when picked indicate secondary folliculitis, triggered when sebum and buildup clog follicles over long periods without washing. The forehead bumps are most likely Malassezia folliculitis (fungal acne) migrating from the hairline via oil. Treating the scalp consistently is the single highest-impact action — healthy hair growth follows scalp health, not the reverse.

Wash Day Routine

Pre-Twist Install Wash Day

Full reset before each new install
  1. Pre-poo — Apply lightweight oil (coconut or olive) to dry hair, 20–30 min before washing. Protects against hygral fatigue.
  2. Cécred Clarifying Scrub — Remove all product buildup. Only do this before a fresh install, not every wash.
  3. Nizoral Shampoo — Apply to scalp, leave 3–5 minutes, then rinse. This is your antifungal treatment step.
  4. Cécred Hydrating Shampoo — Follow-up wash to re-moisturize after Nizoral (which can be drying on its own).
  5. Cécred Reconstructing Mask — Deep condition 20–30 min under a heat cap. Handles protein replenishment.
  6. Cécred Conditioner — Rinse-out conditioner to seal and soften after the mask.
  7. K18 Leave-In Mask — Apply to damp sections. Do not rinse. Small amount per section.
  8. Leave-In Conditioner + K18 Oil — Apply leave-in, then K18 Oil as heat protectant before any blow-drying.
  9. Blow Dry — Low to medium heat only. Stretches shrinkage for length visibility and easier installation.
  10. JBCO on ends — Seal ends and mid-lengths. Apply to edges sparingly. Install twists.

Bi-Weekly In-Twist Wash

While protective style is in — every 2 weeks
  1. Nizoral Shampoo — Focus entirely on scalp. Do not skip this — Malassezia builds up even between installs.
  2. Cécred Hydrating Shampoo — Follow-up, diluted is fine. Focus on twists and ends.
  3. Cécred Conditioner — Focus on ends, leave on 5 min, rinse.
  4. Air dry or diffuse — No direct heat unless reinstalling after this wash.

As Needed — Folliculitis Reset
  • If scabs or burning returns — swap Nizoral for CLn Shampoo

  • Do not use both in the same wash
  • Return to Nizoral once scalp calms
Quarterly Reminder

  • Assess edge health — no tight roots

Non-Negotiable Rules
01
Never Skip Nizoral
You cannot manage seborrheic dermatitis without an antifungal. Nizoral is the evidence-backed standard. Missing it lets Malassezia rebuild faster than any moisturizing product can compensate.
02
Wash Every 2 Weeks Minimum
Your scalp cannot tolerate long gaps. Going weeks without washing is what triggers folliculitis. Protective styles do not excuse skipping wash days — they require them.
03
No Tight Roots on Install
Traction alopecia is permanent. Your hairline is already showing oil sensitivity. Install twists with gentle tension only — if your scalp hurts after 24 hours, they're too tight.
04
Dry Before Sleeping
Sleeping on damp or wet twists creates a warm, humid environment that feeds Malassezia directly. Always air dry or diffuse before bed, especially after wash days.
05
6–8 Week Install Max
Beyond 8 weeks, matting and breakage outweigh the length retention benefits of protective styling. The point is to retain length — not keep the same install until the hair locks.
06
Diet Matters for the Bumps
Seborrheic dermatitis and fungal acne are both worsened by high-glycemic eating. If the forehead bumps persist after consistent scalp treatment, diet is the remaining variable — not more topical products.
