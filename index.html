⚠ Scalp Context — Read First

Your primary condition is likely seborrheic dermatitis — the oily flaking combo is its classic signature. The nape scabs that burned when picked indicate secondary folliculitis, triggered by sebum clogging follicles over long no-wash stretches. The forehead bumps are most likely Malassezia folliculitis migrating from your hairline via oil. Treating the scalp consistently is the single highest-impact action — hair growth follows scalp health, not the reverse.

Wash Day Routine

Pre-Twist Install Wash Day

Full reset before each new install — every 4–6 weeks
  1. 01Pre-poo — Apply lightweight oil (coconut or olive) to dry hair 20–30 min before washing. Prevents hygral fatigue during the wash.
  2. 02Cécred Clarifying Scrub — Massage into scalp and work through hair. Removes all silicone and product buildup before a fresh install.
  3. 03Nizoral Shampoo — Apply directly to scalp, leave 3–5 minutes, then rinse. This is your antifungal treatment step. Do not skip.
  4. 04Cécred Hydrating Shampoo — Follow-up wash to re-moisturize. Nizoral can be drying on its own — this step is always needed after it.
  5. 05Cécred Reconstructing Mask — Apply to wet sections, cover with a heat cap, leave 20–30 minutes. Protein treatment step. Do not skip on install days.
  6. 06Cécred Conditioner — Rinse-out after the Mask. Hair will feel stiff from the protein treatment — this restores softness and moisture balance.
  7. 07K18 Leave-In Mask — Apply dime-sized amounts to damp sections. Do not rinse. Apply before any heat product.
  8. 08K18 Oil — Apply evenly through hair before blow-drying. Thermal protection. Use low-to-medium heat only to stretch shrinkage for installation.
  9. 09JBCO on ends — Seal ends and mid-lengths. Keep off your scalp if SD is active — heavy oils feed Malassezia on an irritated scalp.
  10. 10Install spam twists — Low tension at the roots. If your scalp hurts after 24 hours, they are too tight. Take them down and redo looser.

Bi-Weekly In-Twist Wash

While protective style is in — every 2 weeks minimum, no exceptions
  1. 01Nizoral Shampoo — Focus entirely on scalp. Leave 3–5 minutes, rinse. Malassezia rebuilds even when no visible symptoms are present.
  2. 02Cécred Hydrating Shampoo — Diluted is fine. Focus on twists and ends. Always follow Nizoral with this.
  3. 03Cécred Conditioner — Focus on ends. Leave 5 minutes, rinse. Save the Reconstructing Mask for install days only.
  4. 04Air dry or diffuse on low — No heat unless taking twists down after this wash. Hair must be fully dry before sleeping.
Maintenance Schedule
Daily / Every 2 Days
  • Lightly spritz twists with water + leave-in mix to maintain moisture
  • Apply Cécred Edge Drops to hairline and edges
  • Seal edges with a small amount of JBCO after the drops
Weekly
  • Scalp oil massage: dilute tea tree oil in jojoba, apply between parts
  • Tea tree suppresses Malassezia between Nizoral washes
  • Leave on 20–30 min before washing, or overnight
Every 2 Weeks
  • Full in-twist wash: Nizoral → Hydrating Shampoo → Conditioner
  • Re-moisturize ends and edges after washing
  • If scabs returning: swap Nizoral for CLn this cycle only
Every 4–6 Weeks
  • Take twists down — never keep past 8 weeks total
  • Full pre-install wash day (all 10 steps)
  • Clarifying Scrub to reset all buildup
  • Reinstall fresh spam twists with low root tension
As Needed — Folliculitis Reset
  • Scabs or burning returns: use CLn instead of Nizoral that wash
  • CLn = bacteria. Nizoral = fungus. Never use both same day
  • Return to Nizoral once scalp calms (typically 1–2 CLn washes)
Quarterly Reminder
  • 6–8 week max per install before full takedown
  • Assess edge health — no thinning, no traction pain
  • Restock: Nizoral, K18 Leave-In Mask, JBCO, CLn
Non-Negotiable Rules
01
Never Skip Nizoral
Seborrheic dermatitis cannot be managed without an antifungal. Nizoral is the evidence-backed standard. Every missed wash lets Malassezia rebuild faster than any moisturizer compensates.
02
Wash Every 2 Weeks Minimum
Your scalp cannot tolerate long stretches without washing. Protective styles do not excuse skipping wash days — they require them. The scabs returned because of the gap, not despite the style.
03
No Tight Roots on Install
Traction alopecia is permanent. Your hairline already shows oil sensitivity. Install with gentle tension only. If your scalp still hurts after 24 hours, take them out and redo them looser.
04
Always Dry Before Bed
Sleeping on wet twists creates a warm humid environment that feeds Malassezia directly. Air dry fully or diffuse on low every single time — especially after wash days.
05
6–8 Week Max Per Install
Beyond 8 weeks, matting and breakage outweigh the length retention benefit. The point of the style is to retain length — not to keep the same install indefinitely at the cost of your hair.
06
Diet Drives the Forehead
Seborrheic dermatitis and fungal acne are both worsened by high-glycemic eating. If forehead bumps persist after consistent scalp treatment, diet is the remaining variable — not more products.
